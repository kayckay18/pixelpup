MSPCA-Angell Behavior Department Class Registrations

> User Experience — Technology (Cognito Forms) — User Management

Intro:

Our Animal Behavior Director requested a more simple, streamlined solution that can :

1. Enable pet owners to register for classes

2. Allow anyone on our team to manage the form entries and data to decrease the number of refunds

3. Be compatible with our current CMS, WordPress.

Background:

We were currently using several methods to do one process:

Step 1:

We had an eCommerce store to take payment for class registration.

Step 2:

Step 3:

After that, if they were paying attention a registration form would pull up on top of their confirmation page.

Problem:

There were a lot of dog owners that never filled out the registration form. Even worse, since the form was really long and they only had 20 minutes to complete it, a lot of people would be kicked out before they were finished. Our training coordinators would have to them chase the owners down and ask them to fill out the form for the first time, or again.

In addition, there were quite a few over sells of classes because there were several unrelated user types that would buy classes:

Adopters (those entered in through the adoption center)

Pet owners buying one class

Pet owners buying a "package" of classes.

The original process we had didn't have a way to be able to track one total inventory for all these different types of users

Solution:

After thorough research on a non-profit budget, I was able to find a product that had enough ability for customization, and was within a proper price range. The next step was organizing all the pieces into one beautiful form. This required a lot of time and thought because there were so many packages that would change prices and which classes would be shown for purchase. I had to lay all the parts out in order to see the "logic" each step would need.

After the "pre-planning" for where everything in the form would go and what would happen if they selected each class or package, it was time to get that logic down.

Every part of the form can be customized with your own logic so that the proper information will come up when someone selects certain choices, or enters coupons.

We now have a combined payment and registration in one simple step, in additon we are now able to use logic so that all the seperate user types can pull from the same inventory. Unfortuantely, this project is still currently being worked on, but will be live within the month. The next steps is for me to collect the data with how many oversells we have (if we have any), and if we process less refunds.

MSPCA-Angell Nevins Farm Summer Camp Registration

> User Experience — Technology (Cognito Forms) — User Management

Intro:

Our Camp Program Director came to me asking for an easy, streamlined way for campers' parents to register them for camp that would also be user-friendly for anyone to manage new form builds and entries. In addition, it had to be compatible with WordPress.

Current Process:

We had an eCommerce store to take payment for camp.

After that, they would get an autoresponder that will ask them to remember to go back to the website in order to download the proper forms to finish registration.

These forms would then need to be filled out, signed, and brought in along with vaccinations on the first day.

Problem:

At least one of these pieces would be missed while the parent was registering.

The program director and some volunteers (if she's lucky to have them) would have to chase down parents for their camper's information. Which increses their workload about 20 hours a month.

If they missed a parent and the child didn't have vaccination records then they wouldn't be able to take part in camp, bringing a lot of frustration to parents and counselors alike.

Solution:

After thorough research on a non-profit budget, I was able to find a product that had enough ability for customization, and was within a proper price range, Cognito Forms. The next step was organizing all the pieces into one beautiful form.

Every part of the form can be customized with your own logic so that the proper information will come up when someone selects certain choices, or enters coupons.

Features I've added:

A place to upload vaccination records right away.

Make sure they sign all required documents online, and in one step.

Reactions:

According to the Program Director this form has "changed their lives." They no longer dedicate 20 hours a month chasing people around for their information. They also no longer have to keep track of registration inventory manually, which frees up more time for them to promote their camp.
